# Product Decision Meeting Summary

The team selected **Vendor A (LoyaltyCore)** over the recommended Vendor B (RewardStack) primarily due to time-to-market advantages and financial considerations.

## Key Decision Factors

**Financial Impact:** LoyaltyCore's effective 3-year cost is $2.8M versus $3.3M for Vendor A with supplementary analytics. A critical deadline exists: the 15% early renewal discount expires December 31, 2025, after which prices increase 20%.

**Speed Advantage:** LoyaltyCore requires approximately 5 months to launch, compared to 8+ months for RewardStack. Since customer churn costs roughly $1.1M quarterly, this 3-month difference represents approximately $825K in retained revenue.

**Risk Assessment:** The CTO noted that RewardStack integration with the company's legacy banking system would likely exceed vendor estimates, as has occurred with past integrations.

## Trade-offs Acknowledged

The VP Product advocated for RewardStack's strategic flexibility, stating the platform enables A/B testing of reward structures, whereas LoyaltyCore offers less iteration capability. However, the CEO prioritized immediate market needs: "We're losing customers now. We need to move fast, and LoyaltyCore gets us there sooner with less risk."

## Risk Mitigation

A $200K allocation for a standalone analytics layer addresses LoyaltyCore's reporting limitations while maintaining cost parity and accelerated timeline.

**Launch target:** May 2026, pending December 2025 contract execution.
