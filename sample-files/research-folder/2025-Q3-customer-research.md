# Customer Research Summary — Rewards Program Perception
**Prepared by:** Priya Patel, Analytics Lead
**Date:** September 8, 2025
**Methodology:** Online survey (n=2,847) + 12 focus groups across Toronto, Vancouver, Calgary

## Executive Summary
Customers overwhelmingly want simplicity. The current rewards program is seen as competitive in value but poor in usability. The biggest opportunity is not adding more rewards — it's making existing rewards easier to understand and redeem.

## Key Findings

### Awareness & Understanding
- 62% of customers find the current tier system "confusing" or "very confusing"
- Only 23% can correctly identify which tier they belong to
- 78% don't know their current points balance without checking the app
- Customers who understand the program spend 2.1x more on their card

### Redemption Behavior
- Average time from earning to redeeming: 14.3 months (industry avg: 8.2 months)
- 34% of earned points expire unused (representing ~$4.2M in unrealized engagement)
- Top redemption categories: Travel (41%), Cashback (33%), Gift Cards (18%), Merchandise (8%)
- Customers who redeem within 90 days have 67% higher retention at 12 months

### Competitive Perception
- Our rewards value is rated 3.8/5 (vs. industry avg 3.5/5)
- Our rewards usability is rated 2.4/5 (vs. industry avg 3.1/5)
- Top competitor cited: Simplii Financial (for simplicity), Amex (for value)

### Focus Group Highlights
> "I know I have points but I never know what they're worth. With my Amex, it's simple — one point equals one cent."
> — Focus group participant, Toronto, Age 28

> "I didn't even know I'd been upgraded to Gold. What does that even get me?"
> — Focus group participant, Vancouver, Age 34

> "I'd rather have fewer options that I actually understand than a menu of 50 things I'll never use."
> — Focus group participant, Calgary, Age 31

## Recommendations
1. Simplify to no more than 3 tiers with clear, distinct benefits at each level
2. Implement real-time points balance visibility (push notifications)
3. Introduce "1 point = 1 cent" equivalency for cashback redemption
4. Create 90-day redemption nudges to reduce point expiry
