# Summary: Q1 2026 Rewards Program Restructuring

## Current Status
The rewards program restructuring initiative is progressing but facing a critical compliance delay. The LoyaltyCore contract was successfully renewed in December, and the 3-tier structure (Essentials → Preferred → Premium) is finalized. However, **a legal analysis on points conversion fairness remains unresolved**, blocking the May 2026 launch.

## The Blocker
According to the meeting notes, "We will not approve go-live without resolution of this issue." Specifically, the handling of Silver-tier customers with over 50,000 points requires legal approval before customer migration can proceed. This analysis was due December 15 but remains pending, with a new target of January 31.

## Budget & Resources
The team allocated $350K for Q1 work: $200K for analytics infrastructure and $150K for LoyaltyCore development. A notable decision: leadership selected Metabase (open-source) over commercial analytics platforms, saving approximately $120K.

## Rollout Strategy
The team will phase launch by customer tier—Premium tier first (highest engagement), then Preferred, then Essentials. A 30-day parallel run will keep both old and new systems active during transition.

## Key Risks
Beyond the compliance blocker, WestJet's slow technical response could delay co-brand integration, and customer service must be prepared for potential backlash from tier elimination regardless of conversion fairness measures.
