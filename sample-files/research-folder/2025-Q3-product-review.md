# Product Review — Rewards Program Restructuring
**Date:** August 12, 2025
**Attendees:** Sarah Chen (VP Product), Marcus Williams (Head of Partnerships), Priya Patel (Analytics Lead), James O'Brien (CFO), Lisa Tran (Customer Experience)

## Context
Customer churn in the 18-35 segment increased 14% QoQ. Exit surveys consistently cite "confusing rewards structure" as a top-3 reason for leaving. The current 5-tier system launched in 2021 and hasn't been updated since.

## Discussion

**Sarah** opened by framing this as a retention play, not a growth initiative. The goal is to stop the bleeding in our most valuable demographic before we invest in acquisition.

**Priya** presented the analytics deep-dive:
- Only 23% of customers understand how to maximize their rewards
- 62% find the current tier system confusing (up from 41% last year)
- Customers who actively redeem rewards have 3.2x higher lifetime value
- The "Gold" and "Platinum" tiers have almost identical redemption patterns, suggesting they should be merged

**Marcus** raised concerns about partner impact. Our co-branded card with WestJet depends on the current tier structure. Any changes need 90 days notice per the partnership agreement.

**James** flagged budget constraints. Q4 budget is already allocated. Any new platform investment would need to come from Q1 2026 funds, unless we can make a case for pulling forward.

## Options Discussed

1. **Simplify to 3 tiers** — Merge Gold/Platinum, keep entry tier, create one premium tier. Lowest cost, fastest to implement.
2. **Points-based system** — Replace tiers entirely with a simple points accumulation model. Higher upfront cost but better long-term flexibility.
3. **Hybrid approach** — Keep tiers for status but add a points layer for redemption. Most complex but preserves partner relationships.
