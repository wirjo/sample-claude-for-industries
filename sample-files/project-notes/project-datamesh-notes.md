# DataMesh Enterprise — retrospective notes

## What we did
- Started exploring an enterprise version of our data-platform product
- Targeted at banks and insurers with compliance / data-lineage requirements
- Commissioned industry interviews with 8 potential buyers

## What worked
- Strong initial signal. All 8 buyers identified the problem as real.
- Technical fit assessment from architecture team was positive. We could
  build this.

## What didn't
- Every single one of the 8 buyers had a different idea of what compliance
  required. No common denominator.
- Sales cycle estimates ranged from 9 to 18 months
- Current leadership is not aligned on whether to fund this vs double down
  on FlowSync

## Recurring questions

- **Does a PLG playbook translate to enterprise at all?** Increasingly sure
  the answer is no. The friction is in a completely different place.
- **Is compliance a moat or a tarpit?** Depends entirely on whether the
  buyer understands their own compliance requirements. Eight out of eight
  didn't. That's probably the signal.

## What I decided
- Pause the project. Revisit only if we can find three reference customers
  who are willing to co-design the compliance surface area with us.

## Lessons
- Budget for the cost of ambiguity. If the buyers can't articulate the
  requirement in the same way, the product has to absorb that ambiguity,
  and that's where timelines and scope go to die.
