# FlowSync — retrospective notes

## What we did
- Shipped a cross-tool sync product (Linear + Figma + Notion + GitHub)
- Targeted at product-and-design teams at Series A–C SaaS companies
- Positioned as "one source of truth for what ships"

## What worked
- Linear integration drove trial starts; ~70% of trials used it on day one
- NPS 58 on closed beta (n=42). Best I've seen on anything I've shipped.
- Founders of design-led companies champion the product with zero prompting

## What didn't
- Adoption within a team is bottlenecked on who owns project status. In
  teams where no one clearly owns it, the product never sticks.
- Free tier too generous — several teams never upgraded

## Recurring questions

- **Does "one source of truth" require a deciding authority to enforce it?**
  Looks like yes. Teams without one treat FlowSync as yet-another-tool.
- **Is the ideal user really the PM or is it the founder?** Close call. In
  companies under 40 people the founder still sets status. In companies above
  that size the PM takes over.
- **Should product-led growth serve teams of 15+?** I keep hitting the same
  wall TaskNow did. I'm starting to think the answer is different for
  product-and-design tools versus engineering-only tools. Teams of 20 in
  PD shops seem to adopt FlowSync well; teams of 20 in engineering-only
  shops did not adopt TaskNow. Different dynamics.

## What I decided
- Product-led growth can serve teams up to ~25 in product-and-design shops.
  Going to pursue that segment aggressively. This is a direct revision of
  what I concluded on TaskNow — different market shape, different answer.

## Lessons
- Lessons from one product transfer to another less cleanly than I assumed.
  The team-size ceiling for PLG depends on the market's adoption dynamics,
  not on PLG itself.
