# TaskNow — retrospective notes

## What we did
- Shipped a real-time task collaboration product for remote engineering teams
- Built a Slack-first integration on the premise that Slack = the primary
  channel for async work

## What worked
- The Slack integration drove ~80% of day-one activation
- Onboarding flow (watch a teammate complete a task live) was consistently
  praised in user interviews
- Engineering-only positioning helped us avoid enterprise committee buying

## What didn't
- Product-led growth stalled around team-size 8
- Teams larger than that reverted to Jira or Linear within 30 days
- We kept trying to solve this with features and it never landed

## Recurring questions this project surfaced

- **Should a PLG motion try to serve teams >10?** We kept saying yes. The
  data kept saying no. We never fully committed either way and it showed.
- **Is "Slack-native" a moat or a ceiling?** Slack access broke onboarding
  friction but also capped the ceiling — anyone wanting the work outside
  Slack found us inadequate.
- **Should we have picked a market?** We tried to serve both solo
  freelancers and engineering teams at YC-stage startups. Freelancers paid
  faster; teams stuck longer. We didn't decide.

## What I decided
- Freelancer market is a distraction. Focus exclusively on engineering teams
  up to 10. Clear the decks of freelancer features by end of Q4.

## Lessons
- When the data and the story disagree, the data wins, eventually and
  expensively. Better to listen sooner.
- A narrow market that you can actually win beats a broad market you can't
  serve well.
